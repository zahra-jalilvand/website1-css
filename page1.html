What is a Crime?

A crime is a deliberate act that causes physical or psychological harm, damage to or loss of property, and is against the law.

Crime As Social Problem

Some of us have been directly affected by crime. Many others because we are fearful of it, have been directly victimized by crime.

We therefore routinely process such knowledge, and take defensive action accordingly.

Crimes put heavy pressure on federal, state and household budgets.

Crime is regarded by the public as one of the leading social problems today.

    • Types of crime
    Antisocial behaviour Arson Assault Burglary Child abuse Online crime Domestic abuse Fraud Stalking Terrorism Sextortion
    Antisocial behaviour is when you feel intimidated or distressed by a person's behaviour towards you. Arson is when someone deliberately sets fire to someone else's property to damage it or to injure people. Assault is a violent crime. It's when someone physically attacks you, or threatens to attack you. A burglary is when someone breaks into a building with the intention of stealing, hurting someone or committing unlawful damage. Child abuse can happen in different ways, and can include neglect as well as physical, emotional and sexual abuse. Online crime, or cybercrime, refers to a variety of crimes carried out online using the internet. Domestic abuse describes negative behaviours that one person exhibits over another within families or relationships. Fraud is when someone tricks or deceives you to gain a dishonest advantage. Stalking is fixated, obsessive, unwanted and repeated behaviour that makes you feel pestered and harassed. Terrorist attacks are sudden and unpredictable and generally calculated to create a climate of fear or terror among the public. A terror attack can lead to an ongoing feeling of insecurity. Sextortion, also known as webcam blackmail, is a form of intimate image abuse.
