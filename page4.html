Notes

The 'award' is famously given out each year for the most stupid death, rewarding the person's willingness to “remove themselves from the human gene pool,” and all entries are verified. link1

Hybristophilia is a paraphilia involving sexual interest in and attraction to those who commit crimes. link2

The polygraph, also called 'lie detector', is an apparatus that measures physiological variables such as heart rate, blood pressure, and respiration rate which indicates if a person is being untruthful when asked a series of questions. link3

The Broken Windows theory, first studied by Philip Zimbardo and introduced by George Kelling and James Wilson, holds that visible indicators of disorder, such as vandalism, loitering, and broken windows, invite criminal activity and should be prosecuted. link4

Epidemic hysteria or mass hysteria refers to apparently contagious dissociative phenomena that take place in large groups of people or institutions under conditions of anxiety. link5

The Lead-Crime Hypothesis—that the toxic effects of leaded gasoline explain big spikes in crime—offers convincing evidence and helps explain why violent crime fell so much from the 1990s to today. link6
